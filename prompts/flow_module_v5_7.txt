Use options flow and dark-pool data strictly as a confirmatory continuation / exhaustion layer, never as primary thesis origin.

UNUSUAL ACTIVITY DETECTION LAYER

Before interpreting flow, first decide whether the trade or cluster is actually unusual.

A trade or cluster may qualify as unusual if several of the following are present:
- notional premium is materially large for the name
- relative volume is materially above normal for that strike / expiry
- volume is large relative to prior OI
- follow-up OI confirms fresh positioning
- repeated same-direction prints occur across the same strike / expiry or a logically related cluster
- execution suggests urgency (e.g. Sweeps / ISOs) or persistent repeat demand
- strike / expiry align with a live catalyst window
- activity is idiosyncratic rather than broad sector-wide macro flow

Do not treat a trade as truly unusual based on one metric alone.
Large premium without OI follow-through, or large volume without catalyst alignment, may still be noise, hedge flow, churn, or a roll.

When possible, distinguish:
- unusual single print
- unusual multi-print cluster
- unusual multi-session build

PART 1: OPTIONS FLOW INTERPRETATION

Interpretation hierarchy:
- Open-interest change matters more than same-day volume
- Multi-session build (activity across at least 2 sessions; stronger at 3+) matters more than single-session prints
- Expiry and strike selection must match the catalyst window
- Execution style (e.g., urgent Sweeps / ISOs vs. passive Blocks) or persistent repeat demand across multiple prints matters more than any single print’s bid/ask fill location
- Notional premium and relative volume matter more than absolute contract volume
- Bid/ask/mid location is suggestive, not conclusive
- Flow that looks like hedging, rolling, overwriting, stock replacement, volatility harvesting, or profit-taking is weaker than clean new directional positioning

Reading rules:
- Print near ask may suggest aggressive buying; near bid may suggest aggressive selling; near mid is ambiguous
- Prints above ask or below bid may indicate extreme urgency, quote staleness, fragmented sweep execution, or complex routing; treat them as potentially meaningful but not automatically cleaner than repeated ask-side or bid-side execution
- Do not treat last price location as proof of directional intent
- Always check timestamps for offsetting strikes or related legs to avoid misreading one leg of a multi-leg spread as standalone conviction
- Flow explicitly flagged as tied to stock should be strongly downgraded or excluded from directional confirmation unless additional evidence suggests it is not purely delta-neutral
- If aggressive buying occurs at extremely elevated IV (e.g., IV rank / percentile above roughly 80–90), downgrade directional confidence unless OI build, multi-session consistency, and catalyst alignment are unusually strong
- Volume greater than prior OI may suggest opening activity, but confirmation is stronger if follow-up OI increases
- Rising OI supports fresh positioning; falling OI may suggest closing or unwinding
- Relative volume above roughly 2x–3x normal is more meaningful than ordinary activity
- Strike and expiry should be evaluated for consistency with the catalyst timing and thesis
- Deep ITM calls may reflect stock replacement rather than convex upside speculation
- Far OTM options may reflect lottery behavior rather than informed conviction
- Put activity during a bullish setup may be hedging rather than bearish expression
- If similar flow appears broadly across the sector during the same macro tape, downgrade the idiosyncratic signal value
- If same-day options flow conflicts with a stronger multi-session OI build, prefer the multi-session read unless the same-day activity clearly reflects a material unwind, reversal, or contradiction confirmed by price action

Open-interest lifecycle rules:
- Use day-over-day OI change to distinguish likely opening, holding, adding, reducing, and closing activity
- High volume on a given day does not prove fresh opening if OI later fails to rise or subsequently falls
- If large volume appears after an earlier OI build and OI then declines, consider whether the activity was closing, reducing, rolling, or profit-taking rather than new conviction
- If OI rises across multiple sessions, treat this as stronger evidence of sustained position building
- If OI peaked several sessions ago and has since fallen materially, downgrade the idea unless current flow shows a credible new rebuild
- When possible, interpret current flow in the context of the recent OI path, not just today’s prints
- If current volume is large but OI has already been declining for several sessions, assume the default read is more likely exit / unwind than fresh opening unless other evidence is unusually strong
- If OI falls while price continues in the expected direction, consider whether the move is being driven by closing flow rather than fresh conviction
- If OI rises again after a prior decline, distinguish a true rebuild from noisy churn by requiring multi-session consistency or clear catalyst alignment
- If OI on a previously supportive strike or expiry declines materially, check whether positioning appears to be migrating to a logically related strike or later expiry before treating it as a true unwind
- If OI is declining on the original line but rebuilding on a replacement strike or later expiry consistent with the same thesis, treat the original line as a roll or migration rather than a full exit
- If OI declines without credible replacement flow, treat the prior supportive build as partially or fully unwound and downgrade flow confirmation accordingly
- If the likely lifecycle state is unclear, include that explicitly under Downgrade factors

PART 2: DARK POOL / EQUITY BLOCK INTERPRETATION

Interpretation hierarchy:
- Price action relative to the dark-pool print matters more than the print itself
- Multi-day dense clusters at a specific price zone matter more than isolated random blocks
- Relative size versus 30-day average daily volume matters more than raw shares alone

Reading rules:
- Do not infer trade direction from dark-pool execution alone
- Never assume a dark-pool print is inherently a buy or sell; treat it first as a large off-exchange transfer of risk
- Treat significant dark-pool levels as structural support / resistance or magnetic pivot zones
- A major dark-pool level generally means clustered activity large relative to normal trading, for example roughly 5% or more of 30-day ADV
- Bullish confirmation: price structurally holds and sustains above major dark-pool levels
- Bearish confirmation: price structurally breaks and sustains below major dark-pool levels
- A massive print near the high of an extended move followed by price rejection may suggest distribution
- A massive print near the low of an extended selloff followed by price bounce may suggest accumulation
- Account for late reporting; many dark-pool prints should be mapped as structural levels, not immediate momentum signals

PART 3: ADDITIONAL CONTEXT RULES

Dealer gamma / GEX context:
- If dealer gamma exposure context is available, use it as a secondary impact check, not a thesis originator
- Positive gamma environments may damp directional price impact from flow
- Negative gamma environments may amplify directional price impact from flow
- In a clearly negative gamma environment, temporary breaks of dark-pool structural levels may reflect dealer-driven price acceleration rather than clean thesis failure
- If this appears likely, treat it as a downgrade and reassess using multi-session OI build, catalyst alignment, and subsequent price stabilization or recovery
- Do not let negative gamma automatically override a genuine structural breakdown
- If dealer gamma context is unavailable, state: Data unavailable — verify in R2
- Do not let GEX override cleaner evidence from catalyst alignment, OI build, price action, and dark-pool structure

Time-of-day context:
- Consider when the flow occurred
- Opening-session flow may reflect institutional hedging, macro reaction, or event positioning rather than pure idiosyncratic speculation
- Closing-session flow may reflect rebalancing, hedging, or end-of-day positioning rather than fresh directional conviction
- Quiet-session midday flow may carry more idiosyncratic signal than broad opening or closing rotations
- If flow occurs primarily in the first or last 30 minutes of the session and no immediate catalyst is present, apply a soft downgrade unless other evidence strongly supports fresh idiosyncratic conviction
- Do not over-interpret time-of-day in isolation; use it as a context modifier only

Confluence rule:
- Alignment between major dark-pool structural levels and directional options flow at the same price zone is one of the strongest confirmation patterns in this framework
- Misalignment between dark-pool structure and options flow should downgrade conviction
- Directional options flow occurring while price is holding the correct side of major dark-pool levels is stronger than similar flow occurring against those levels

Signature-print / late-print proxy:
- If a delayed dark-pool print is clearly outside the current session’s range, treat it as a prior-session structural level rather than a real-time momentum signal
- If current price is materially above that late print, it may imply prior institutional accumulation below the market
- If current price is materially below that late print, it may imply prior institutional distribution above the market
- Use this as a secondary directional proxy only; do not override cleaner price-action evidence or stronger multi-day structural levels
- If timing or print status is unclear, include it under Downgrade factors

Expiry-week / pin-risk context:
- In the final 1–2 trading days before expiration, high-OI strikes and dealer hedging can distort price action and weaken directional inference
- Near expiry, apparent support / resistance or unusual flow may reflect pinning, hedging, or expiry-related positioning rather than fresh conviction
- If the setup is inside this expiry window, downgrade tape confidence unless the catalyst is immediate and the flow / price-action alignment is unusually clean
- If expiry-related distortion is likely, include it under Downgrade factors

PART 4: SYNTHESIS / DOWNGRADE PROTOCOL

If flow or dark-pool interpretation is mixed, ambiguous, unsupported by OI change, unavailable, or directly contradicts price action, do not let it rescue a weak idea.

Supportive conditions generally require some combination of:
- stock trading on the correct side of major dark-pool levels
- multi-session directional consistency in options flow
- supportive OI build
- expiry alignment matching the catalyst window
- strike placement logically consistent with the thesis
- execution mechanics suggesting urgency (e.g., Sweeps / ISOs) or persistent repeat demand across multiple prints
- premium size / relative activity that is meaningfully unusual, e.g. notional premium or volume materially above the recent norm for that strike / expiry when available
- isolated rather than obviously paired / hedged / spread-driven prints
- supportive dealer gamma context when available
- constructive confluence between options flow and dark-pool structure
- supportive signature-print context when clearly identified and not contradicted by cleaner real-time price action
- supportive OI lifecycle context showing fresh build, sustained holding, credible rebuild, or clear migration / roll rather than likely exit flow

Downgrade factors may include:
- no follow-up OI confirmation
- obviously paired or spread-driven prints
- tied-to-stock or likely delta-neutral structure
- extremely elevated IV without unusually strong supporting evidence
- sector-wide macro flow reducing idiosyncratic signal value
- contradictory price action relative to major dark-pool levels
- flow concentrated at expiries or strikes that do not match the catalyst window
- dark-pool data or options-flow data that is stale, delayed, or structurally unclear
- time-of-day suggesting broad hedging/rebalancing rather than fresh conviction
- dealer gamma context that dampens expected directional impact when available
- possible gamma-driven dislocation through dark-pool structural levels
- likely expiry-week pinning or hedging distortion
- signature-print timing or interpretation is unclear
- OI lifecycle suggests likely unwind, reduction, or churn rather than fresh opening / build
- previously supportive OI build is materially unwinding without credible migration to a replacement strike or later expiry
