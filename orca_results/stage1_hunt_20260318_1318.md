Why this company is the cleanest expression:
Cheniere Energy is the leading producer and exporter of LNG in the United States and second largest producer of LNG globally. Cheniere has one of the largest liquefaction platforms in the world, consisting of the Sabine Pass and Corpus Christi liquefaction facilities on the U.S. Gulf Coast, with total production capacity of approximately 45 million tonnes per annum of LNG in operation. No other listed US single name has comparable direct exposure to the global LNG supply shock. While Morningstar notes the contracted structure, Q4 2025 actual EPS was $10.68 versus a $3.80 estimate, a 181% surprise, demonstrating Cheniere captures far more upside from tight global LNG markets through trading, optimization, and uncontracted volumes than the consensus narrative assumes.

European natural gas prices surged, with the benchmark Dutch TTF April 2026 gas contract jumping about 29% from the settle at the end of last week to just shy of €70/MWh as the Iran war carried into its second week. Crude prices are up around 40% since the United States and Israel attacked Iran on February 28. Brent topped $109 today.
