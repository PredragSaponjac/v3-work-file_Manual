Why this company is the cleanest expression:
Cheniere's terminals are on the Gulf of Mexico coast, entirely outside the Strait of Hormuz. While Qatar's production is offline and Middle Eastern supply chains are paralysed, Cheniere's facilities are operating normally, with contracted cargoes loading and departing on schedule. Cheniere has one of the largest liquefaction platforms in the world, consisting of the Sabine Pass and Corpus Christi liquefaction facilities on the U.S. Gulf Coast, with total production capacity of approximately 45 million tonnes per annum of LNG in operation and an additional 10+ million tonnes per annum under construction. Beyond contracted volumes, Cheniere's marketing and trading arm profits from LNG market dislocations and volatility — as demonstrated by fourth-quarter 2025 adjusted net income of $2.302 billion or $10.68 per share, up 147% from $977 million or $4.33 per share in Q4 2024, partly reflecting favorable derivative and market-volatility gains. The current Hormuz crisis represents far more severe market dislocation than Q4 2025 conditions.

What already moved:
Europe's benchmark Dutch TTF futures dropped as much as 12% to below €48/MWh from as high as €56/MWh, following an extraordinary bout of volatility that saw prices nearly double over 48 hours. TTF remains sharply elevated relative to pre-crisis. The worst supply disruption in the history of the oil market is showing no signs of abating anytime soon, with crude prices that have surged 40% since the Iran war began. About 20% of global liquefied natural gas exports that come from the Persian Gulf are also at risk, primarily those originating from Qatar and shipped via the Strait of Hormuz. QatarEnergy has been forced to halt production at the world's largest single-site urea plant, while Indian manufacturers have cut output and Bangladesh has curtailed operations.

What has already repriced in this stock (and approximate magnitude):
A 30-day share price return of 15.32% and a 90-day gain of 27.40%. Stock trading near $252–254 as of March 13. The 90-day gain includes the pre-conflict Q4 2025 earnings beat and $10B buyback announcement, so the conflict-specific move is roughly the 15% 30-day return.
